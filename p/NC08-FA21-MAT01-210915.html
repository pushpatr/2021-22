If 21y5 is a multiple of 9, where 'y' is a digit, what is the value of y? 1×2M = 2Marks

Divisibility rule of 9 states, "A number is divisible by 9 if the sum of all the digits of the number is a multiple of 9."

Hence the sum of all digits of given number '21y5' must be a multiple of 9.

So, \[ \begin{align} 2+1+y+5 &= 9 \text{ or } 18 \text{ or } 27 \text{ or } 36 \text{ ...}\\ 2+1+5+y &= 9 \text{ or } 18 \text{ or } 27 \text{ or } 36 \text{ ...}\\ \therefore 8+y &= 9 \text{ or } 18 \text{ or } 27 \text{ or } 36 \text{ ...} \end{align} \] Hence, \[ \begin{align} 8+y&=9 &\text{ OR ­ ­ ­ ­ ­ ­ ­ ­} 8+y&=18 &\text{ OR ­ ­ ­ ­ ­ ­ ­ ­} 8+y&=27\\ y&=9-8 & y&=18-8 & 8+y&=27-8\\ y&=1 & y&=10\text{(Not a digit)}\\ \end{align} \]

Therefore 'y' can only take the value of 1 for the number 21y5 to be a multiple of 9.

\(\therefore y=1 \) and number is 2115 (\(9 \times 235 = 2115\)).
